Upgrade request: finer quantization grid for humanization preservation
Context: I've been converting more SOUN files and listening to the output. The current 16th-grid quantization is producing a "locked in" / "on rails" feel across whole tracks — uniform across all voices, not specific to drums. This is humanization loss, not swing (offbeats aren't landing wrong; everything is just too perfectly on-grid).
My earlier diff of mi_scummbar.mml showed emberling used only power-of-two durations with one exception (a single r32). I took that to mean 16th-grid was sufficient. After listening, I think what that diff actually shows is that emberling simplified aggressively when transcribing, not that the iMUSE source material was that clean. The raw MIDI has finer timing than his MML preserves.
Requested change: raise the quantization grid from 16th to 32nd by default, with 64th available as an option for tracks that need it. Keep 16th as an option for cases where a clean transcription is preferred over fidelity.
What this should NOT include: triplet detection or swing quantization. My diagnostic is that offbeats are landing correctly, just stiffly — the problem is grid resolution, not rhythmic feel. Adding triplet heuristics would be speculative and out of scope.
Calibration approach: run before/after on a specific SOUN that sounds locked-in at 16ths. If 32nd-grid preserves the life without producing %N fragment spam in the output, it's working. If outputs are cluttered with %5 / %7 / %11 tick values, the quantizer needs to be smarter about collapsing near-grid-aligned hits.
Implementation note: one risk is that dropping to 32nd-grid will produce messier MML — more ties, more small fragments. The existing quantize_and_clean function already merges same-pitch contiguous events, but at finer resolution there will be more borderline cases. Worth adding a "grid tolerance" parameter: hits within ±N ticks of a grid line snap to it, hits further out get their own slot. This keeps the output clean for music that's close to 32nds while preserving genuine micro-timing where it exists.
