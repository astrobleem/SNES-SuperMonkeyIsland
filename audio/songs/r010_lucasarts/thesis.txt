r010_lucasarts — arrangement thesis
====================================

What this actually is
---------------------
Despite the `room_010_logo` filename, this is NOT the LucasArts fanfare —
this is the Secret of Monkey Island *main theme* that plays over the title
screen / attract mode. Source is `soun_005.bin` in the logo room, presumably
stashed there because the LucasArts logo intro runs into the MI title sequence
without a room transition.

Source character
----------------
  * 11.87 measures, 4/4, TPB 480
  * 8 MIDI channels active + drums on ch9 (822 hits — dense)
  * Only 0.3% of melodic onsets land on a 16th grid → deeply iMUSE-performed
  * Instruments (GM program, voice count):
      ch1  186  Shakuhachi      (lead)
      ch2  240  FX 8 (sci-fi)   (atmospheric wash)
      ch3  640  Glockenspiel    (twinkle harmony, highest note count)
      ch4  549  Sitar           (plucked ornament)
      ch5  192  Alto Sax        (low D1-G2 — original bass voice)
      ch6   42  Acoustic Bass   (walking bass — primary)
      ch7   51  Fiddle          (countermelody)
      ch9  822  Drums           (Side Stick / Kick / Conga Open+Mute / Claves)

Mood / palette intent
---------------------
Mystical pirate reggae. Tropical, adventurous, slightly wistful. The tribal
percussion (kick + side stick + hi conga alternation) drives the reggae
pocket; shakuhachi + glockenspiel + sitar layer the harmony; acoustic bass
walks underneath; fiddle weaves in and out as a melodic counterpoint.

Arrangement decisions
---------------------
  * Grid: 32nd + tolerance 1 MML tick. The 0.3% 16th alignment rules out
    snap-everything without flattening the performance. 32nd preserves the
    shuffle feel; tolerance 1 collapses near-grid near-misses so the output
    stays readable.
  * Playback clock: `#Timer 256` at `#ZenLen 192`. Source MIDI reads 30 BPM
    meta but iMUSE tempo metas are noisy — matching the existing
    hand-arranged `audio/songs/soun_010.mml` clock gives ~1:14 playback,
    which sounds right against the reference.
  * Voice allocation (8 of 8 voices, structure follows the prior
    `soun_010_plan.json` that produced recognizable output):
      A  lead @shakuhachi_high     (ch1 top)       _M-1
      B  pad  @glockenspiel_high   (ch3 top — bell chord top)
      C  pad  @glockenspiel_high   (ch3 bottom — bell chord bottom)
      D  arp  @sitar_high          (ch4 top)       _M-18
      E  bass @acobass             (ch5 top — "Alto Sax" prog IS the bass)
      F  doubler @atmosphere_high  (ch2 top — FX 8 as the pad)          _M-13
      G  ornament @shakuhachi      (ch7 top — fiddle countermelody)     _M-17
      H  drums @kick/snare/conga_hi/claves/bongo_hi                     (ch9)
  * ch6 "Acoustic Bass" is NOT the bass line — its MIDI range F#4-D5 is
    a mid-register plucked part. The actual bass line is ch5 "Alto Sax"
    (D1-G2). This was a lesson learned: GM program names in iMUSE MIDI
    don't reliably tag musical role.

Palette — SUPERGUYBRUSH soundbank (ADL FM patches rendered to BRR)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
The SOUN resource ships THREE parallel music streams bundled in one
container: ROL (Roland MT-32 GM MIDI), GMD (General MIDI), and ADL
(AdLib). The ADL stream has identical note content and channel layout
to the ROL version, but instead of `program_change` events it ships
inline `F0 7D 10 <ch> <26-byte patch> F7` sysex messages carrying the
2-op FM instrument definitions (modulator/carrier ADSR, waveform,
multiplier, feedback, connection).

We decode those patches (`tools/adl_patches.py`) and render each by
driving a REAL OPL2 emulator (the pyopl binding to MAME's OPL2/OPL3
chip emulation). The 26-byte patch becomes a direct register-write
sequence — AM/VIB/KSR/Mult, KSL/TL, Waveform, FB/Connection — with
the envelope bytes overridden to force a stable sustained spectrum
(EG-type=sustained, AR=15, DR=0, SL=0, RR=0). Envelope timing stays
in TAD's per-note ADSR. Output is trimmed to an integer number of
periods with phase-matched loop endpoints so BRR loops click-free.
Drums (ch9) are rendered as band-filtered noise bursts per drum role
(kick/snare/hat/crash/tom/conga/claves/cowbell/woodblock/triangle/shaker).

The result: every SNES voice on this track plays a tone derived
directly from the data MI1 fed to real AdLib hardware in 1990. No
orchestral-sample emulation, no shared instrument bank — a
one-of-a-kind FM palette unique to this song, forged from its own
source bytes.

Samples (8 channels, all in `samples/`):
  r010_fm_ch1  additive,  mod×1 car×7  FB=4  → lead (shakuhachi-equivalent)
  r010_fm_ch2  FM,        mod×1 car×1  FB=1  → FX 8 pad (doubler slot)
  r010_fm_ch3  FM,        mod×1 car×3  FB=0  → glockenspiel (bright 1:3 bell)
  r010_fm_ch4  FM,        mod×1 car×1  FB=0  → sitar (arp)
  r010_fm_ch5  additive,  mod×1 car×0.5 FB=6 → bass
  r010_fm_ch6  additive,  mod×1 car×9  FB=0  → (unused; ch6 acoustic-bass is a mid-pluck line we dropped)
  r010_fm_ch7  additive,  mod×1 car×7  FB=4  → fiddle (ornament)
  r010_fm_drum noise burst + carrier envelope → drum kit (all GM drums map to this single patch)

No per-song synth.py; the FM rendering is driven by
`tools/adl_patches.py <mid> --out-dir samples/` and is reproducible
from the source .mid alone.

Triplets / swing
----------------
The source has reggae-adjacent rhythmic displacement but no triplet figures
detected in a quick scan (no notable onset cluster at tpb/3 = 160 ticks).
Handle via grid tolerance, not triplet bracket notation.
